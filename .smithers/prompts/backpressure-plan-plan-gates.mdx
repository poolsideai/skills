# Plan the backpressure gate matrix

You are the gate-planning step of the "backpressure plan" pipeline. Take the
extracted acceptance criteria and turn them into a **gate matrix** — a verification
method and enforcement policy for each criterion — so a downstream workflow cannot
just try its best and move on. Every criterion needs a way to be proven.

## How to plan each gate

For **every** criterion, emit one gate object:

- `criterion` — the criterion text this gate enforces.
- `verificationMethod` — pick the cheapest method that actually proves it:
  `schema` (validate output shape), `unit_test`, `integration_test`, `eval`
  (LLM-judge / scorer), `review` (agent diff review), `approval` (durable human
  gate), `trace` (assert on recorded execution/telemetry), or `manual_check`
  (a human eyeballs it) only when nothing automatable fits.
- `gateType` — `blocking` stops the run on failure, `warning` flags but continues,
  `informational` only records. Default correctness and safety criteria to
  `blocking`; reserve `warning`/`informational` for nice-to-haves.
- `checkedBy` — who or what runs the check: a task id, a named scorer, a human
  role, or a tool/command.
- `failureAction` — exactly what happens when the gate fails (retry the task, halt
  the run, route to a human, open a follow-up, etc.).
- `evidenceRequired` — the concrete artifacts that prove it passed: test output,
  a diff, a coverage report, a recorded trace, an approval record. Never accept
  "the agent said it was done" as evidence.
- `humanApprovalRequired` — `true` only when a durable human approval is genuinely
  needed for that criterion.

Rules:

- **Every blocking criterion MUST map to a real verification method** — no blocking
  gate may be left as a bare assertion with no way to check it.
- Prefer automatable methods (`schema`, `unit_test`, `integration_test`, `eval`,
  `trace`, `review`) over `manual_check` and `approval`; only escalate to a human
  when a machine genuinely cannot decide.
- Do not drop or merge criteria — one gate per criterion, same order as the input.

Finish with a 2-3 sentence `summary` of the overall backpressure posture (how many
gates are blocking, where humans are in the loop, the weakest spot).
